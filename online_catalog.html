Online Catalog

Programmatic catalog capture from major retailers' websites
What this will do

Weekly snapshots of in-store-eligible products from major full-price retailers: Walmart, Target, CVS, Walgreens, Sephora, Ulta. Plus DIY scraping of value-tier retailers Family Dollar, Dollar General, Dollar Tree, and Five Below.

Captured per product: brand, name, pack size, retail price, list price, promotion text, UPC, image URL, in-stock status, store availability. Full price history tracked so you can chart retailer pricing moves over time.

Two-stage approach
StageRetailersMethodMonthly cost
Stage A — DIYFamily Dollar, Dollar General, Dollar Tree, Five BelowDirect HTML fetch + Claude parsing$5–30
Stage B — ApifyWalmart, Target, CVS, Walgreens, Sephora, UltaApify scrapers + Claude normalization$80–200
In-store filter (critical)

At Walmart and Target, the captured catalog is filtered to "in-store eligible" items only via store-pinned URLs. Walmart's 100,000+ marketplace SKUs in beauty are excluded. The captured catalog represents what the retailer itself sells AND has stocked at the chosen store — directly comparable to TBG's in-store shop-outs.

Cross-source queries this enables
  • Biggest price gap between off-price in-store and full-price online — margin opportunities
  • Products in dollar-store catalogs currently trending on social — value-tier validation
  • MFG vendor codes from shop-outs appearing under different brand identities online — supplier intelligence
  • Items at full-price retailers that haven't yet appeared in TBG's off-price shop-outs — pipeline signals
