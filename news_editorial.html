News & Editorial Monitoring

Editorial signal capture across six tiers of publication
What this will do

Most beauty and HBC trade publications publish RSS feeds. The platform polls them daily, sends each new article through Claude for entity extraction, and stores structured mentions of brands, products, ingredients, retailers, and factories — all linked into the same cross-source canonical registry as the other intelligence modules.

The result is a queryable, time-stamped feed of every relevant mention across the editorial landscape, automatically tied to TBG's existing brand, product, and factory records.

Six tiers of source

Different publications carry different signal types at different authority weights. Trade press is the most reliable for factual B2B intelligence. Celebrity press carries weaker authority but the strongest mass-consumer demand signal.

Why celebrity press despite low authority weight

Authority weight measures factual reliability. Celebrity press carries a different signal: mass consumer demand. When People features a $8 drugstore mascara that's "selling out everywhere," that product will appear in TBG's shop-outs within weeks. This is a leading indicator of shelf demand — especially relevant for HBC sourcing that skews drugstore and mass tier.

Cross-source queries this enables
  • Brands mentioned in Beauty Independent in the last 30 days that are also in TBG's factory pipeline — sourcing opportunities
  • Ingredients trending up over the last 6 months in editorial coverage — formulation roadmap
  • Trends spotted in factory intel that later get editorial validation — confirmation signal
  • M&A and supplier consolidation affecting TBG's competitive set or supply base
  • Brand crises (recalls, ingredient controversies) on competitors TBG sources against — opportunistic positioning
